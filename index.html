A Seminar on Art of Living

A serious problem of superstition and witch hunting prevailing in certain pockets of Assam leading to search for witches or evidence of witchcraft, often involving moral panic, mass hysteria and lynching Superstition is a belief in supernatural causality: that one event leads to the cause of another without any physical process linking the two events, such as astrology, omens, witchcraft, etc. that contradicts natural science. The word is often used pejoratively to refer to religious practices other than the one prevailing in a given society although the prevailing religion may contain just as many supernatural beliefs. In it innocent people are hacked to death by their fellow villagers. The reason? They were all suspected of practicing black magic or witchcraft. Though government has introduced a draft bill to tackle the social evil of witch hunts. The state Govt yet to take any legal action to try and curb the practice.

To stimulate and to educate the women folks the people of Panidihing in the Sivasagar district against the superstition and witch hunting, NEST foundation has conducted one day seminar among the tribal women of remote locality at Katiyari Higher Secondary School, of the area. Dr. Bina Baruah, Head of Department of Botany and Smt Manjumani Das , Lecturere of chemistry Sivasagar College Assam has delivered a excruciating lecture on ill effects of superstition and witch hunting. In continuation of their lecture they have briefed the audience of advancement in Science & Technology of 21st century and how one can live with dignity without giving any heed to such superstitions.

Dr. Sabir Parvez Islam , has briefed about the advancement in Medical Sciences & its technology and its utilization to prevent unforeseen diseases which are misnomers to superstition. It was stressed that prevention is better than cure.

Special emphasis was given Art of Living. Unless we have a stress free mind and a violence free society, we cannot achieve word peace and personnel growth. Through Art of Living one can have stress-elimination and self development programmes based mainly on breathing techniques, meditation and Yoga. These programmes have helped thousands around the world to overcome depression and violent and suicidal tendencies. A range of mental and Physical benefits were discussed including reduced levels of stress, improved immune system, relief from anxiety and depression and increased antioxidants protection, enhanced brain function among other findings.

The audience was engrossed with the new findings.

Shri P.R.Mili, Chairman NEST Foundation, presided over the seminar.
Shri P.K.Das, Life member of NEST foundation anchored the program.

Help to Flood affected people

The flood situation in Assam worsened on 28/06/2012. With new areas coming under water and the toll reaching 22. The current flood was biggest since 1998 as opined by different agencies.
Altogether, 21 districts have been affected following the week-long incessant rains. The river has breached embankment at five places, affecting 1,744 villages across nine district and 70,000 hectare of crop land. The river island Majuli was the worst sufferer. About 70% of the island had been affected.

The administration has opened relief camps, where nearly 2 lakh people have taken shelter. The state government sought more relief material.

NEST Foundation, a volunteer NGO, came forward to extend whole heartedly support for the flood affected peoples. Well wishers and members voluntarily collected the different usable items which are bare minimum requirements at this juncture and handed it over to flood affected people at relief camp opened at Dikhowmukh High School under Sivasagar District Assam.

Following Items were distributed at this relief camp.

1. Four No. of Tarpaulins of 15'/20' size.
2. Fifteen Hundred (1500) good conditioned clothes.
3. Thirty (30) plain new Mekhla sadar for 30 old women.
4. One Full cartoon of mosquitoes repellant.
5. Five Hundred(500) Tooth Brush.
6. Two Hundred (200) Colgate tooth paste.
7. Two Hundred (200) Bathing soaps.

Help to another flood affected people on 30th June, 2012

Observed 65th Death Anniversary of one of the eminent freedom fighter and ex Forest Minister of Assam Late Bhimbor Deori on 30th November, 2012

Born in Deori Borgaon, Nitaipukhuri, Panidehing in 1903; a very remote village in Sivasagar District, late Bhimbor Deori was ex-forest minister of Assam. But he was more of a leader of the people and for the people. Bhimbor Deori was a freedom fighter and a renowned lawyer from the state of Assam in India. A stern nationalist from the indigenous society of Assam played a significant role on the eve of the change of colonial power in Assam. He was one of the main architects of the famous "Khasi Darbar Hall Resolutions" held on 21,22 and 23rd March 1945 where indigenous leaders of different ethnic identities resolved resolutions to restore their independent homelands against Indian occupation He led the history of north east a new turn and vision to look ahead with a prime development and with essence of freedom to self independence of different tribes He was founder General Secretary of Assam Plain Tribal League in the year 1933. He tried hard to ensure that indigenous people of the State to allotted the land pattas.

But today, the people of Assam have forgotten this multi-faceted leader and son of Assam.

NEST foundation observed 65th death anniversary to commemorate this silent hero at the premises of Rangpur Jyoti Club, Sivasagar and made the young generation acquainted with his qualities.

PREAMBLE OF THE TRUST

The NEST Foundation believes in the philosophy of " Atta Deepo Bhava " i.e. Enlighten Yourself . education is the master key of the development of the individual, society and Nation as well. education is also basis of awakening, self reliance and healthier and better living. the Founder Members felt to establish the Foundation for the development, establishment, improvement, betterment, settlement and management of environment, communication, rural development, social works, science and technology, planning and entrepreneurship for providing better living to the people and contributing in the Nation building. It is felt that issues related with intellectual property rights are getting prominence in the world, but in India, it is required to educate, to create awareness in the people of India about the issues, rights and duties related with intellectual property as Patents, Copyrights, Trademarks, Geographical Indications and Trade Secrets etc. Protection, preservation and promotion of the traditional knowledge, traditional cultural expressions, traditional folklore and indigenous knowledge of the people of India is also a need of the hour and also to safeguard the interest, rights and benefits of the holders of holders of the traditional knowledge traditional cultural expressions, traditional folklore and indigenous knowledge of the people of India. For the development and growth of the country, it is felt that the Young People of India should be provided training and career related program so that they can stand on their feet and can contribute for the Nation as well. Women empowerment and child care issues and also the health of the common people is utmost important for vibrant and modern India In order to achieve all these, the NEST Foundation will work tirelessly and will generate resources.


AIMS AND OBJECTS OF THE TRUST


1. To establish Public School, College, Institute and University at various places in order to impart modern and useful education to children beloning to poor, needy, underprivileged and downtrodden people.

2. To establish, maintain, run, develop, improve, extend, grant, donate for and to aid in the establishment, maintenance, improvement and extension of Public School, College, Institute and University in the field of Arts, Science, Commerce, Music, Engineering, Medical, Management, Law, Intellecutual Property Rights, Traditional Knowledge, Biodiversity, Wild Life Conservation and in any branch of the knowledge.

3. To institute studentship, scholarships, Stipends, medals and prizes etc. to help and encourage deserving students and to provide monetary aid to students, scholars, teachers for pursuing their studies.

4. To organize examinations and other competitive activities to select the best talent available among socially, economically and educationally backward society. the selected candidates would be given all facilities and guidance to carry out futher study in India or abroad wherever the Trust shall decide. This help may be in the form of cash, scholarship, award, coaching, guidance etc. The Foundation shall create awareness in masses through organizing camps and seminars etc. at different placed in the country to achieve this objective.

5. To educate, create awareness in the people of India about the issues, rights and duties related with intellectual property as Patents, Copyrights, Trademarks, Geographical Indications, Trade Secrets and others and also to protect and promote Intellectual Property Rights through Audio-Visual Media, Print and electronic media and through various other forms like art, painting, crafts etc.

6. To protect, preserve and promote the traditional knowledge, traditional cultural expressions, traditional folklore and indigenous knowledge of the people of India.

7. To safeguard the interest, rights and benefits of the holders of the traditional knowledge, traditional, cultural expressions, traditional folklore and indigenous of the people of India.

8. To protect, preserve and promote the traditional handicrafts, traditional handlooms, traditional paintings, traditional art, traditional food, beverages and other things related with indigenous and traditional knowledge of the people of India.

9. To protect, preserve flora fauna, biodiversity and wild life and to educate, to create awareness about the issues related with them through Mass Media and other activities.

10. To promote traditional therapies like Yoga, Naturopathy, Meditation, Acupressure, Acupuncture, Massage Therapy, Ayurveda, Homeopathy, Reiki etc.

11. To open women and child care centre and also to organize the family welfare programmes and to carry out the activities related with woman empowerment and development.

12. To use Right to Information (RTI) Act for social upliftment, rooting out corruption in India and to fulfill aims and objectives of the Trust.

13. To provide education and various trainings to the handicapped persons and also to conduct lectures, seminars and other programmes to them.

14. To make donation, grants, loans and other aid to various branches of the public schools, educational institutions and public charitable institutions run by the foundation.

15. To grant endowments at universities, research institutions, educational and scientific institutions for spread of education and knowledge in all fields and any branch of knowledge.

16. To acquire immovable and movable properties and let out the surplus properties for furtherance of objectives of the Trust.

17. To develop the rural society through education, employment and providing information, knowledge in the field of agriculture and day to day activities and to establish such scheme and programmes for the development of the land for agricultural purpose and also to develop and use the means and methods for "Harit Kranti" in India.

18. To protect environment, create awareness about the issues related with environment through education and mass media activities.

19. To establish charitable hospitals, health camps and to organise medical camps and such other activities for betterment of the health of the people and to create awareness and upgrade consciousness for better health and family planning.

20. To propect, preserve and promote the culture, traditions and language of the people of India and in order to protect, promote them establising offices, institutes and others as per requirements.

21. To promote and protect natural herbs and plants by cultivation, preservation, storage, processing, demonstration, creating botanical garden and providing necessary training.

22. To develop human resource and to conduct various career oriented programmes, computer training and Information Technology (IT) related courses for the students, unemployed persons and needy people to provide them livelihood, and to meet day to day challenges for the growth of the Nation and also to issue the Certificate of Completion of the Training/Career Oriented Programme to the participants.

23. To practice and promote Yoga, Meditation, Pranayam and other forms of the art of living and to issue 'Certificate of Completion of the Programme'.

24. To restore the human rights and duties among the masses against police atrocities, social discrimination, communal riots and caste discrimination etc and to educate, to create awareness about human rights and duties and also to promote them.

25. To provide vocational trainings, guidance and extend all help to encourage the self employment and commercial activities for self reliance and better living and also to issue the 'Certificate of Completion of the Training' to the participants.

26. To provide consciousness among deprived and have-nots for their self respect.

27. To propagate the philosophy of action and reason as against the philosophy of fatalism, inaction, discrimination and orthodoxy.
